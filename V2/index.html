How are we different?

Collaboration with SJA and BHA

We collaborate with BHA and SJA to help protect the environment on Jeju Island. Together, we address pressing issues such as pollution, deforestation, and coastal erosion by combining resources and expertise to make a bigger impact. Whether through cleanups, tree planting, or educational campaigns, we work to raise awareness and drive sustainable change in the community.

Prioritizing education and awareness

Our community service group stands out by collaborating with other organizations, prioritizing education and awareness, and using an online store to raise funds. We are dedicated to creating a sustainable impact on Jeju Island through our various actions and programs.

Environmental Problems and Solutions

Jeju Island is a beautiful place with a unique natural environment, but like many areas, it faces a number of environmental challenges. Here is a list of some of the major environmental issues facing Jeju Island, along with potential solutions:

1

Overdevelopment and loss of natural habitat

As the island's population and tourism industry have grown, there has been a trend towards overdevelopment and loss of natural habitat. One solution to this problem is to implement more stringent zoning laws and development regulations that prioritize the protection of natural areas.

read more

Pollution from industries

Industrialization and urbanization have led to increased pollution on the island, particularly in the form of water and air pollution. To address this issue, it is important to implement stricter environmental regulations and enforce penalties for companies that violate them.

read more

Litter and waste management

Jeju Island generates a large amount of waste, which can be harmful to the environment if not properly managed. One solution is to increase recycling efforts and implement more effective waste management policies and infrastructure.

read more

Water scarcity

As the island's population and tourism industry have grown, there has been increasing pressure on the island's water resources. To address this issue, it is important to implement more effective water conservation measures and explore alternative water sources, such as desalination.

read more

Invasive species

Jeju Island is home to a number of invasive species, which can be harmful to native plants and animals. To address this issue, it is important to implement effective control and eradication programs to prevent the spread of invasive species.

read more

Coastal erosion

Coastal erosion can be caused by a variety of factors, including rising sea levels, storms, and human activities. To address this issue, it is important to implement measures to protect the coastline, such as building sea walls, planting vegetation, and enforcing regulations on development in areas prone to erosion.

By addressing these environmental issues, we can work towards a more sustainable and healthy future for Jeju Island.
